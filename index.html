Core functions

Built for low-maintenance urban sensing.

The project focuses on a practical environmental monitoring node that can collect meaningful local data while reducing dependence on wired power and Wi-Fi infrastructure.

🌫️

Air quality sensing

Measures PM2.5 and PM10 to observe local particulate pollution conditions in outdoor environments.

🌡️

Weather context

Records temperature, humidity and air pressure to give more context to changes in air quality data.

📡

LoRaWAN communication

Sends compact data packets over a long-range, low-power LoRaWAN connection to support outdoor deployment.

☀️

Solar powered

Uses a solar panel, rechargeable battery and duty-cycled operation to support autonomous sensing.

System architecture

From outdoor sensing to indoor display.

1

Sense

The outdoor sensor node measures particulate matter and environmental conditions at regular intervals.

2

Transmit

The node sends a compact payload through LoRaWAN, reducing power consumption while maintaining long-range communication.

3

Receive and display

A dedicated display node presents live readings and selected historical values on an e-ink screen.

Why LoRaWAN?

Long range, low power, no Wi-Fi.

LoRaWAN is suitable for sensing systems that send small amounts of data over long distances. For this project, it helps the outdoor node operate away from mains power and local Wi-Fi networks.

E-ink display node

A low-power screen for people, not just data logs.

The display is part of the system rather than a separate exhibition prop. It turns sensor data into an accessible public-facing output, showing current air conditions alongside recent historical readings.

Readable in bright spaces

E-ink provides a paper-like surface that remains easy to read under ambient light.

Low energy demand

The screen only needs power when the image changes, making it appropriate for a solar-powered system.

Current and recent values

The interface shows real-time measurements and a compact history, helping viewers understand environmental change over time.

Prototype summary

Designed as a self-sustained sensing layer.

The prototype explores whether small, solar-powered LoRaWAN nodes can support scalable neighbourhood-level environmental monitoring.

Sensing node Outdoor low-power microcontroller with particulate and environmental sensors.
Communication path Sensor node → LoRaWAN gateway → decoded environmental readings → display node.
Deployment intent Off-grid monitoring for urban sites where wired power and Wi-Fi are limiting factors.

A compact system for seeing local air change.

Solar power supports autonomy. LoRaWAN supports distance. The e-ink display makes the data visible and understandable.
